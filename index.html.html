Vehicle Classifier · charge the right category, every time

Not listed? Follow the rules in order

Work top to bottom. The first rule that fits wins.
1
Double cab overrides everything. Any four-door bakkie with two seat rows (Hilux, Ranger, D-Max, Amarok). Select Double cab, charge SUV price.
2
Passenger minibus / taxi (Quantum, Sprinter, Transit Minibus). Select Minibus taxi.
3
Low compact people-movers are Crossovers, not SUVs. Car height, no real ground clearance, 5 to 7 seats (Avanza, Rumion, Ertiga, XL6, Triber, Xpander, BR-V). If it sits low like a car, it is a Crossover even with 7 seats.
4
Raised 7-seaters are SUVs (Fortuner, Everest, MU-X, Prado, Land Cruiser, Sorento, Santa Fe, XUV700). High ride, off-road stance.
5
Low-roof hatch, sedan, coupe or wagon (Polo, Corolla, Swift, C-Class). Select Small car.
6
Compact raised vehicle (Magnite, Fronx, Jolion, Tiggo 4, Corolla Cross). Select Crossover.
7
Medium or large raised passenger vehicle (Tucson, H6, RAV4, X3, Q5). Select SUV.
8
Private people carriers are normally SUVs (H-1, Staria, Carnival, V-Class, Caravelle) unless clearly a taxi.
9
Single cab bakkies (NP200, single-cab Hilux): ask first, are we washing the load bin? No = Small car price. Yes = SUV price. Never charge SUV price for a cab-only wash.
10
Still unsure? Classify by the physical vehicle (height, length, seat rows, load bin). Use the nearest benchmark or ask management.
